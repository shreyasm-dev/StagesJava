Tsiolkovsky's rocket equation (also called the Ideal Rocket Equation) states that:

Δ v = v e ln m 0 m f

where

  • v is the change in velocity (ms)
  • ve is the exhaust velocity (ms)
  • m0 is the initial mass (kg)
  • mf is the final mass (kg)


Isp is a more convenient way to express the exhaust velocity, rather than the raw exhaust velocity.

v e = I sp g 0

where
  • Isp is the specific impulse (s)
  • g0 is the standard gravity (constant, approximately 9.81) (ms2)
